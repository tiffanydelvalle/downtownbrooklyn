DOWNTOWN BROOKLYN BEAUTIFICATION BUDGET TO IMPACT BUSINESSES

Tiffany Del Valle
October 6, 2023

Downtown Brooklyn responds to Mayor Eric Adams’ proposed $40 million plan to improve the neighborhood. The plan promises street safety, improved public space, and is an initiative in collaboration with New York City Department of Parks and Recreation Commissioner Sue Donoghue and Department of Transportation Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez. Specifically, there will be a streetscape improvement project along Fulton Street which should be adding trees and outdoor furniture such as benches and tables. The investment is supposed to revitalize one of Brooklyn’s largest economic hubs which includes Fulton Mall, City Point, Dekalb Market, and other local businesses. The beautification project will continue to encourage more consumers and shoppers from across the borough to visit and get their commercial and shopping needs addressed. The business district encompasses an array of different business types that will benefit from additional foot traffic in the neighborhood.

BUSINESSES IMPACTED BY BEAUTIFICATION BUDGET

Restaurants

There are 194 places to go eat including bakeries, coffee shops, bars, fast and casual food, full service, and pizza. Roughly 20% of these are located at Dekalb Food Hall in City Point.

Retail

There are 155 places to shop in Downtown Brooklyn and more than half of them are shops to buy consumer goods such as clothing, beauty items, shoes, apparel, and jewelry.

Grocery

There are 25 locations where you can purchase grocery iteams in the neighborhood including a Trader Joe's that is expected to get more foot traffic as the area continues to beautify.

Hospitality

There are 10 hotels in the neighborhood, accomodating tourists who also utilize public space and support businesses in the area. In terms of leisure, there are 29 businesses that fall into arts culture, entertainment, and theaters.

Data source: Downtown Brooklyn Partnership
